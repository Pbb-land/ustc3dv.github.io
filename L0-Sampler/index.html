L0-Sampler: An L0 Model Guided Volume Sampling for NeRF

Liangchen Li          Juyong Zhang
University of Science and Technology of China    
Paper Code

We introduce the L0-Sampler, an upgraded version of NeRF's Hierarchical Volume Sampling (HVS) strategy. Through the use of piecewise interpolation, we fit a quasi-L0 w(t). By normalizing this function, we create a PDF that effectively guides inverse transform sampling. With minimal impact on training time and straightforward code modifications, our method consistently enhances rendering quality, making it a valuable addition to the NeRF framework.

Abstract

Since being proposed, Neural Radiance Fields (NeRF) have achieved great success in related tasks, mainly adopting the hierarchical volume sampling (HVS) strategy for volume rendering. However, NeRF's HVS approximates distributions using piecewise constant functions, which provides a relatively rough estimation.

Based on the observation that a well-trained weight function w(t) and the L0 distance between points and the surface exists very high similarity, we propose L0-Sampler by incorporating the L0 model into w(t) to guide the sampling process. Specifically, we propose to use piecewise exponential function rather than piecewise constant function for interpolation, which can not only approximate quasi-L0 weight distributions along rays quite well but also can be easily implemented with few lines of code without additional computational burden. By applying L0-Sampler to NeRF and its related tasks like 3D reconstruction, stable performance improvements can be achieved.

Method

During volume rendering, the color weight function w(t), exhibits a unique property known as the "L0 property": In scenes where a surface S is present, most points in space have a density of 0, except for the surface points. Consequently, the weight of points along the ray takes on a similar form to the L0 distance between the points and the surface. To incorporate the L0 property into the weight function, we employ a method of interpolation. In this way, we obtain a quasi-L0 function that preserves the L0 property to a certain extent while also considering residual space optimization.

The figure above is an overview of our L0-Sampler pipeline. The red dash line presents the surface. During hierarchical volume sampling, we first uniformly sample points as NeRF in coarse stage, and then through piecewise interpolation, we fit a w(t) resembling an indicator function, which is in line with the L0 distance between points and surface. After normalization, it can be used as a PDF to guide inverse transform sampling. Sampling frequency in each interval (right) shows that our method can make the sampling more focused near the surface.

Results

L0-Sampler demonstrates consistent performance enhancements when integrated with NeRF and related tasks like 3D reconstruction.
